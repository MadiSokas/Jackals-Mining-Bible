4.7 Operations Hub

Daily Briefing

Welcome, Jackal. In 4.7, raw vendor sales are dead as a primary income loop. Our mission is to feed the Org's Tier Zero Fabricator with high-quality ore. Everything you mine should be evaluated against one question: does this rock score 700+ quality? If yes, extract. If not, move on. High-quality minerals crafted into gear are worth multiples of their raw sell price.

Priority: Quantanium

Status: VOLATILE

Best surface deposits on Lyria (Crusader) and Pyro III. 15-minute volatility timer from first extraction. Start timer immediately, plot refinery route before cracking.

Priority: Bexalite

Status: STABLE

Primary Fabricator input ore. Target 700+ quality scores only. Best concentrations at HUR-L3 asteroid belts (Stanton) and Pyro I — Fairo surface deposits.

New in 4.7: Multi-Mineral Rocks

Status: ACTIVE

Rocks now carry up to 3 ore types each with their own quality score. Evaluate all sub-materials before deciding to skip. A 900+ sub-material on a low-value primary rock is still worth extracting.

New in 4.7: Breaker Stations

Status: LIVE

QV Breaker Stations in the Nyx/Keeger Belt unlock team-based ops with exclusive rewards. Requires Fighter + Miner + Crafter + Hauler roles. See the Breaker Station Guide for full walkthrough.

System: Nyx

Status: ACTIVE

Home of Levski (full refinery hub) and the Breaker Stations. Glaciem Ring suits solo fast-grab runs. Keeger Belt has Quantanium with less competition than Pyro III. No UEE law. Always base at Levski first.

Recruit Training

The 4.7 Golden Rules

Rule 1 — Quality beats everything. A 1000-quality Titanium rock is more valuable to the Org than a 0-quality Quantanium. Quality score (0–1000) directly determines crafting output from the Fabricator. Target 700+ on every ore, every run.

Rule 2 — Rocks have multiple ores now. In 4.7 each rock carries a primary ore plus up to 2 sub-materials. Check all three scores before deciding to skip. See the Multi-Mineral Rocks section for the full decision framework.

Rule 3 — The Fabricator Loop is the mission. Raw vendor sales are a fallback. Mine → Refine → Fabricate → Supply the Org. Every high-quality batch you bring in translates to better gear for Jackal combat pilots.

1. Scanning

Ping (TAB) to find clusters. Get within 200m to resolve full stats: Quality score, Resistance, and Instability. Don't crack blind — always resolve composition first.

2. Evaluate the Rock

Check primary quality score. Check both sub-materials. Run the Rock Eval Checklist mentally or use the interactive checklist in the Missions & Org section. Skip anything under 300.

3. Fracturing

Keep the laser in the GREEN band. If it spikes RED your cargo explodes. Use Rime module to dampen instability. Deploy a gadget (BoreMax or Sabir — never both) before firing on difficult rocks.

4. Extraction

After fracture, use minimum power to pull fragments into cargo. Rushing the extraction phase causes overcharge and secondary detonation. Each sub-material enters cargo as a separate stack.

5. Transport

Quantanium: 15-minute timer starts on first extraction. Haul direct to refinery — no stops. All other ores: stable, but watch for pirates. Escort recommended on Pyro or belt runs.

6. Refine & Deliver

Submit to refinery using Dinyx Solventation for max yield. Collect refined boxes and haul to the Fabricator terminal. Sort batches by quality score before submitting — don't mix 900+ with 300- in the same job.

Use Regolith.rocks for current I-Type asteroid locations. Note: Asteroid signatures changed in 4.7 — ice asteroids previously looked like salvage panels on scanner.

🪨 Breaker Station Asteroid Drops — Savrilium & Torite

When the Breaker Station laser fires and cracks open the asteroid, the interior contains Savrilium and Torite as the primary minable ores — confirmed by MrKraken's 100% yield guide. Higher laser alignment (more refractors aligned) means deeper drilling and better quality drops. These are not exclusively Pyro ores — they are accessible via the Nyx Breaker Station without needing to travel to Pyro. Refine at Levski before hauling.

🌑 Sadaryx — The Nyx-Specific Material

Sadaryx is not sold on any market — it has no aUEC value. Its only use is fabricating the three laser lenses required to complete a QV Breaker Station run. Each lens requires 4 Sadaryx; you need 3 lenses per run (12 minimum, 15+ recommended). Delamar caves and the Glaciem Ring's derelict QV stations are the most reliable source. Always stock up before heading to a Breaker Station contract.

Quality Score Guide

The 0–1000 System (4.7)

Every minable material in 4.7 now rolls with a quality score from 0 to 1000. This score directly determines crafting value — not just vendor price. A low-quality Quantanium is nearly worthless for fabrication. Stop chasing rare ores blindly. Chase high-score ores.

Field Decision Rule

Quick call when scanning a rock:

Quality < 300 → SKIP IT
Quality 300–699 → Vendor sale only — fill cargo if nothing better nearby
Quality 700+ → EXTRACT — this feeds the Fabricator Loop
Quality 900+ → PRIORITY EXTRACT — flag for Org supply run

Remember: a 700+ common Titanium rock beats a 200-score Quantanium for crafting purposes every time.

Multi-Mineral Rocks (4.7)

What Changed in 4.7

Rocks no longer contain a single ore. In 4.7, each rock can contain a primary material plus up to two sub-materials, each with their own quality scores and percentages. This changes how you evaluate every rock you scan.

After fracturing, each sub-material enters your cargo hold as a separate stack with its own quality rating. Sort before refining — don't mix high and low quality batches into the same refinery job.

The Fabricator Loop

What Is It?

The Tier Zero Fabricator is Star Citizen's first player-driven crafting system, introduced in 4.7. It lets players manufacture FPS gear — weapons, armor, and components — directly from refined minerals. The Jackals' mining operation exists to feed this loop. Raw vendor sales are secondary.

⚠️ 4.7 Crafting Scope — Not All Materials Are Active Yet

Every resource now carries a quality score (0–1000), but only some materials feed into blueprints in 4.7. CIG has confirmed the intention is for every resource to eventually enter the crafting loop in later patches. Currently confirmed blueprint ingredients include: Taranite, Gold, Copper, Aluminum, Bexalite, Iron, Titanium, Silicon, Pressurised Ice, Savrilium, Torite, and several others. Mine everything at 700+ quality — even materials not yet in blueprints will be added. Don't discard high-quality batches of uncommon ores just because they aren't in a blueprint today.

The Full Loop

1

Extract High-Quality Ore

Target 700+ quality rocks. Multi-mineral rocks count — any sub-material above 700 is useful.

2

Refine at a Refinery Deck

Submit ore for refining. Choose your method — Dinyx Solventation gives the best yield for most ores. Pay the processing fee; get refined material boxes out.

3

Transport to a Station with a Fabricator

Haul refined boxes to a station with a Tier Zero Fabricator terminal. Currently available at major orbital stations in Stanton.

4

Craft Components & Gear

Use the Fabricator terminal to select a blueprint. Insert your refined materials. Quality score of input directly affects the stat roll on the crafted item.

5

Equip, Sell, or Supply the Org

Crafted gear goes to Jackal combat pilots, sold on player markets, or stockpiled for Org operations. High-quality outputs command a premium over NPC vendor gear.

Every minable material has a risk profile. These ratings cover volatility, instability, explosion force, and pirate target risk. Use this before planning a run to pick the right modules, gadgets, and crew size.

💥 Explosion MultiplierHigh — asteroid interior is confined
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone — but haul to Levski before jumping
📈 InstabilityModerate — manageable with correct modules
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskExtreme — Shared contracts have active PvP

Protocol: Exclusive contracts only until your crew knows the layout. Fighter covers exterior. Refine at Levski before leaving Nyx. Never haul raw Torite or Savrilium through a jump point without refining first.

💥 Explosion MultiplierModerate
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone — stable ores
📈 InstabilityModerate — use Surge, watch the bar
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskHigh — valuable cargo attracts attention

💥 Explosion MultiplierLow — stable ores
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityLow to moderate
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskExtreme — Pyro-exclusive ores = high-value target

🟡 MEDIUM — Gold / Laranite / Agricium / Hephaestanite / Diamond / Atlasium / Dymantium

💥 Explosion MultiplierLow–Moderate
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityLow — forgiving extraction window
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskMedium — valuable but not their first choice

Protocol: Good training ores for new Jackals. Stable enough to practice laser control. Still worth an escort in Pyro or Nyx due to smarter AI pirate flanking behaviour. High quality scores on these make them worthwhile Fabricator Loop inputs.

🟡 MEDIUM — FPS / ROC Gems (Hadanite, Aphorite, Dolivine, Saldynium, Jaclium, Beradom, Marok Gem)

💥 Explosion MultiplierNone — no laser, hand-mined
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityNone — personal inventory risk only
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskMedium — known gem locations attract griefers

Protocol: No explosion risk from the mining itself. Main danger is surface exposure and ambush at known cave sites like Daymar. Use stealth — disable transponder before landing at high-value gem locations. ROC runs pair well with a wingman for surface coverage.

🔴 LEGENDARY — Janalite

💥 Explosion MultiplierNone — FPS hand-mined
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityNone
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskExtreme — ~77M aUEC/SCU makes you the highest value target on the server

Protocol: If you find Janalite, do not broadcast it in open comms. Secure extraction immediately. Fly with an armed escort directly to a TDD. Do not stop. Do not detour. This is a "drop everything" Org priority event — alert Jackals privately and get coverage fast.

🟢 LOW — Titanium / Aluminum / Copper / Iron / Tungsten / Corundum / Quartz / Ouratite / Cobalt / Tin / Silicon / Beryl

💥 Explosion MultiplierMinimal
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityVery Low — wide optimal window
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskLow — not worth their time

Protocol: Solo-safe everywhere. Good for quality-farming runs and training new Jackals. Don't write off 900+ quality common ores — a high-quality Titanium or Cobalt batch has real Fabricator value. These also make excellent sub-material fillers when paired with epic ore primary rocks.

🟢 SAFE — Ground Minerals (Glacosite / Feynmaline / Carinite) / Ice

💥 Explosion MultiplierNone
⏱️ Volatility TimerNone
📈 InstabilityNone
🏴‍☠️ Pirate Target RiskNone — not a target

Protocol: Completely safe to mine solo. Ground minerals are situational — low value but useful for specific crafting recipes. Ice asteroids in Pyro refine into Pressurised Ice needed for 4.7 crafting blueprints. Use Regolith.rocks to locate I-Type ice asteroid signatures.

Breaker Station Guide

What Is It?

Operation Breaker Stations takes players to the Keeger Belt in Nyx, where Shubin's recently acquired QV Breaker stations sit abandoned and unpowered, their rock-cracking capabilities long dormant. These missions come in either an Exclusive PvE format or an open FFA PvP format. Pick up the contract at Nyx in the Contract Manager under Ship Mining.

⚠️ Reputation Gate

Access to Breaker Station mining claims requires a minimum reputation level with the relevant faction before contracts appear. Build rep by completing standard contracts in the area first. If you don't see Breaker Station contracts in your Contract Manager, your reputation isn't high enough yet — run standard ship mining contracts in Nyx until they unlock.

🔒 Exclusive Contract

Private instance. Interior is secure from other players. Exterior landing area may still have PvP. Best for Org runs learning the layout.

⚔️ Shared Contract

Up to 5 groups share the same station simultaneously. Expect hostile player contact anywhere on site. Higher risk, higher reward pressure. Bring a fighter escort.

Before You Deploy — Kit Check

🌡️ Temperature armour (Pembroke or Morozov set)Required
🔧 Multitool with Mining, Salvage and Tractor Beam attachmentsRequired
💊 Stims, medpens, respawn-capable shipRequired
🔫 Ripper SMG + extra ammo (enemies are numerous)Recommended
🏪 Buy gear at Levski before jumping to NyxBest Practice
📍 Set ship respawn at a nearby station before enteringBest Practice

💡 Set personal waypoints as you explore. The facility is large and easy to get turned around in. Note refractor codes in the format A-1234 as you find them.

Full Mission Walkthrough

PHASE 1 Land & Breach

With no hangar access, the only entry point is a landing pad next to the maintenance port. From there, suit up and make your way inside on foot, navigating icy caverns to locate the central power core.

  • Land behind the large rock at the ring's landing pads
  • Enter airlock, use corner elevators to reach the Power Core level
  • The Claw Salamanders — deep-space scavengers — and Valakkar in a new icy variant roam the caverns. Clear them before switching to your Multitool
  • Break ice walls with your Mining Tool (full power), repair broken doors with your Salvage Tool

PHASE 2 Restore Power & Collect Sadaryx

Locate the derelict yellow mining robots in the Power Core area. Harvest their capacitors and install them at the central Power Core.

  • Restoring power activates CCTV consoles throughout the station — these hold the refractor codes
  • While clearing this area, collect Sadaryx crystals from tunnels — they look like white geodes near blue Aphorite deposits
  • You need at least 12 Sadaryx total — each lens takes 4, and you need 3 lenses. Grab extras for future runs
  • Clear ice blockages in tunnels with your Mining Tool or grenades. Watch for fauna in the dark
⚡ Collect more than 12 Sadaryx. Having spares eliminates a major bottleneck on repeat runs.

PHASE 3 Facility Reset & Collect Refractor Codes

Board the tram to the Operations facility. A full Facility Reset is mandatory — codes will not appear until it is triggered.

  • Enter the Operations room airlock, hit the reset button on the primary console
  • Three randomised refractor codes (A, B, C) now appear at three locations:
  • 📍 Power Core — bridge below the capacitor turn-in
  • 📍 Operations — right of reset button by the server rack
  • 📍 Storage — inside the Storage sector (accessed later via Laser section tram)
  • Write all codes down. Format: A-1234 / B-5678 / C-9012

PHASE 4 Fabricate Lenses at the Laser Section

The laser emitter is accessible by tram or by flying directly to it — it sits above the service station where you first landed.

  • Find the lens fabrication machine near the entrance (right side)
  • Load 4 Sadaryx into the hopper slot → hit the button → fabricate one lens. Repeat 3 times
  • Use your tractor beam to move each finished lens to the front of the emitter. Insert them into the armatures — numbers above each lens change from red to white when properly seated.
  • If the machine bugs, use your Tractor Tool to pull materials back out and run it again
  • Also collect the Storage sector trolley key and the Refractor A code here if not already found
🔧 Lens bay is up the stairs on the opposite side of the laser from the storage trolley. Easy to miss on first run.

PHASE 5 Align Refractors A, B & C

Board the external tram. Navigate to Refractors A, B, and C via the tram navigation screen.

  • Each refractor has two terminals — enter the code on either side
  • Screen turns green to confirm acceptance. If it throws an error, check the code again
  • More lenses and mirrors aligned means a more powerful beam, deeper drilling, and access to rarer resources.

PHASE 6 Load Catalysts & Fire the Laser

Go to the laser control cab at the back of the emitter, up a set of stairs. The display shows power, lenses, refractors, and catalysts — the catalysts are missing.

  • Retrieve the lasing catalysts from the Storage depot (trolley ride from Phase 4)
  • Return to the laser control cab, load all catalysts, confirm all four status lights are green
  • Fire the laser. The asteroid cracks open

PHASE 7 Mine the Asteroid Interior

Descend into the cracked rock and extract what's inside. The two primary ores are Savrilium and Torite — confirmed by MrKraken's 100% yield guide as the key Breaker Station drops.

  • 100% yield requires all refractors aligned — skipping any refractor reduces ore quality and quantity. Don't shortcut Phase 5.
  • Savrilium and Torite are the primary ores. Quality scales with laser alignment — more mirrors = higher quality score on drops
  • Haul extracted ore back to your ship using the tractor beam setup. Refine at Levski before jumping out of Nyx
  • Blueprints are earned from completing the contract — they are the exclusive reward, not a unique ore. Check the contract terminal after completion
  • Loot room keycards found on enemies and in containers unlock bonus loot rooms — always check bodies and crates
  • Use the proximity looting system (I key → box icon) to cycle containers fast
🏆 Rewards: Savrilium + Torite ore (quality scales with alignment) + exclusive Fabricator blueprints + aUEC payout. Full 100% yield run with all refractors aligned can reach millions per session.

Mining Lasers Deep Dive

All ship mining lasers fall into two sizes. Size 1 fits the Prospector. Size 2 fits the MOLE (three arms). Choosing the right laser for the job changes how every rock feels to crack.

📦 Size 1 Lasers — Prospector

Arbor MH1 — Greycat Industrial

Size 1 Safe / Balanced 2 Module Slots

The standard laser on most ships by default. Solid, reliable, and forgiving. Filters out inert material, reducing junk in your cargo. Good balance of speed and safety. Best entry-level laser for new Jackals.

Jackal Use: Default loadout for training runs and general Stanton ops.

Hofstede S1 — Shubin Interstellar

Size 1 Low Instability 2 Module Slots

Dampens resistance while mining and collects less inert material. Short-to-medium range only. Higher instability than the Arbor — requires active management — but the resistance reduction is excellent for dense deposits. Best for high-value quality runs where you want clean extractions.

Jackal Use: Primary recommendation for Bexalite and Taranite quality farming.

Impact I — Thermyte Concern

Size 1 High Power 2 Module Slots

Increased power with a wider optimal charge window. Deposits overheat faster and explode harder if you lose control. The improved optimal window makes it more forgiving for intermediate miners who can manage the heat. Good for resistant rocks where the Arbor stalls.

Jackal Use: Resistant rock specialist. Pair with Rime module on volatile ores.

Helix I — Thermyte Concern

Size 1 Very High Power 3 Module Slots

The most powerful size-1 laser. Three module slots — the most of any size-1 head — makes it extremely customisable. Throttle responsiveness is slower, so overshoots happen fast. Explosions hit significantly harder when they do occur. A sign of a skilled Jackal miner when used correctly.

Jackal Use: Expert-level. Best for Quantanium and high-resistance epic ores with Rime + Surge + Brandt combo.

Lancet MH1 — Greycat Industrial

Size 1 Support / Low Power 2 Module Slots

Support laser designed for multi-ship ops. Generates a weak beam that safely reduces instability and resistance but cannot crack a rock solo. Pairs with a second Prospector or runs alongside a MOLE arm. Ideal when one operator controls stability while another drives the fracture.

Jackal Use: Support role in 2-Prospector ops or as a stability controller in dual-laser runs.

📦 Size 2 Lasers — MOLE

Arbor MH2 — Greycat Industrial

Size 2 Safe / Balanced 2 Module Slots

The size-2 version of the reliable Arbor line. Same inert material filtration and balanced approach as the MH1. Good default for the MOLE when a crew is learning the ropes or doing lower-risk ore runs.

Jackal Use: MOLE default and training arm. Reliable on Gold and Laranite runs.

Hofstede S2 — Shubin Interstellar

Size 2 Safety Focused 2 Module Slots

The size-2 safety laser. Dampens instability to dramatically reduce explosion risk. Sacrifices range. The go-to choice for any MOLE arm running Quantanium where one accidental explosion can wipe the whole team.

Jackal Use: Mandatory on Quant MOLE runs. At least one arm should always run an S2 on volatile ops.

Helix II / III — Thermyte Concern

Size 2 Very High Power 2–3 Module Slots

Size-2 versions of the powerful Helix line. Impressive power and range for hard rock fracture. Throttle lag and increased explosion force means operators need experience. The Helix III adds a third slot for more configuration. Best results on Bexalite, Taranite and asteroid belt rocks.

Jackal Use: MOLE primary arm. Helix III is the top-tier MOLE laser for serious belt runs.

Impact II — Thermyte Concern

Size 2 High Power 3 Module Slots

High power with a wider optimal window and three module slots. Increases deposit resistance as a tradeoff. Slower charge window fill. The three slots give flexibility to counter the resistance penalty. Good versatile MOLE arm that doesn't require expert-level throttle control.

Jackal Use: Versatile MOLE arm for mixed-ore runs. Impact II + Brandt + Focus + Surge is a strong all-rounder combo.

Klein S2 — Shubin Interstellar

Size 2 Fast / Aggressive 2 Module Slots

Speed-focused size-2 laser. Mines fast but boosts instability and explosion risk as a result. For careful operators who want to maximise runs-per-hour on lower-resistance ores. Not recommended for Quantanium or high-instability rocks.

Jackal Use: Speed run arm for common ore quality-farming. Swap out before Quant ops.

Lancet MH2 — Greycat Industrial

Size 2 Support Only 2 Module Slots

Size-2 support laser for multi-crew MOLE operations. Reduces instability and resistance on a rock while the power arms fracture it. Assign your least experienced crew member to this arm — it's nearly impossible to blow a rock with it alone.

Jackal Use: Dedicated stability arm on MOLE. Assign to support role in 3-person crews on volatile rocks.

Mining Gadgets

Gadgets are single-use consumables deployed directly onto a rock before or during fracturing. They can dramatically alter rock behaviour. Critical rule: never use more than one gadget per rock. Two gadgets on the same deposit causes a guaranteed catastrophic explosion.

💣

Sabir — Resistance Breaker

Uses a sensor to modulate power and adjust it to the specific deposit. Dramatically drops resistance and widens the optimal charge window — makes hard rocks significantly easier to crack. Increases instability as a tradeoff.

Best for: Highly resistant rocks (Bexalite, Taranite, high-resistance Quant)

⚠️ Only one gadget per rock. Never combine with BoreMax.

🔵

BoreMax — Instability Reducer

Focuses laser power to significantly reduce instability while keeping deposit clusters together on shattering (better fragmentation = better yield). Makes bypassing resistance slower and more difficult. Most miners find the safety and yield improvement worth the trade.

Best for: Volatile ores (Quantanium), high-instability rocks, inexperienced crews

⚠️ Only one gadget per rock. Never combine with Sabir.

⚡

Stalwart — Power Precision

Designed for miners who want to wield high power precisely. Helps experienced operators hit and hold the optimal window on very powerful lasers like the Helix. Narrow operating window — not recommended for beginners.

Best for: Expert operators running Helix + Surge + Rieger combos on hard rocks.

⚠️ Only one gadget per rock.

⛔ The One Gadget Rule

This is not a suggestion. Using two gadgets on the same deposit triggers an immediate, unavoidable explosion regardless of where the power bar is. The explosion will destroy your cargo, damage or destroy your ship, and kill your crew. There are no exceptions. One gadget maximum per rock, per crack attempt.

Cargo Protocols

📦 The Quality-to-Market Pipeline

How we handle assets is dictated by their grade. These are not suggestions — treat them as law to ensure the Org scales effectively. The quality grade maps directly to the Mining Bible's 0–1000 quality score system.

Alpha — Legendary / Tier-1 (900–1000)

Protocol: DO NOT SELL.
Must be transported directly to the Jackal Home Base or designated Division Hangars. These materials are heavily restricted and reserved exclusively for internal Fabricator crafting and base fortification. Alert the Crafter role immediately on extraction.

Beta — High Grade (700–899)

Protocol: INTERNAL REFINING ONLY.
Move directly to industrial processing hubs. Pilots receive a 5% payout bonus for delivering to Org-contracted refining operations instead of standard market dumps. Primary Fabricator Loop input.

Gamma — Industrial Grade (400–699)

Protocol: MARKET CLEARANCE.
Authorized for immediate liquidation at any available Trade Terminal. These keep the day-to-day credits flowing for active members. Sell at Lorville TDD or New Babbage for best rates.

Delta — Raw / Impure (0–399)

Protocol: BULK DUMP.
Only worth moving in massive shipments. Recommended as zero-risk training runs for new recruits to practice flight lanes and tractor-beam maneuvers. Do not prioritise over higher grade runs.

Loading & Efficiency Standards

⏱️ The 30-Minute Rule

If a loading operation at an outpost takes longer than 30 minutes, the risk of a hostile scout locating your position increases significantly. Be prepared to abandon half-filled grids and execute an immediate extract if the clock runs out.

🚀 Ship-Specific Standards

C2 Hercules: Prioritise 32 SCU containers only. Do not waste time stacking small boxes. Speed over precision.

Freelancer MAX: Preferred hull for running high-value Alpha materials through contested corridors — smaller signature reduces interdiction risk.

Fleet Convoy Formations

Never fly blind. When moving heavy assets, utilise the following assigned roles to ensure survival against hostile interdictions. Every formation needs all three roles filled before departure.

⚔️ The Vanguard (Security)

Flies point. Leads the quantum jump 10 seconds ahead of the rest of the fleet. Primary objective: scout the destination for pirates and clear any active interdiction snares before the haulers arrive. Calls "Comms Clear" when zone is secure.

📦 The Asset (The Hauler)

Stays dead center of the formation. Never drops shields for any reason. If pulled out of quantum, the Asset holds course and prepares to burn toward a backup emergency waypoint while escorts engage. Calls "Contact Front" on any hostile detection.

🛠️ The Tail (Support/Salvage)

Follows 5km directly behind the main Asset. If the hauler goes down, the Tail is responsible for suppressing remaining hostiles and recovering floating cargo boxes using on-board tractor beams. Last ship out of any hostile zone.

🔒 Formation Rules

All three roles must be filled before a high-value convoy departs. No exceptions for Alpha material runs. MOLE extraction runs to Levski require at minimum a Vanguard escort. Never quantum jump without confirming all fleet members are in formation on comms.

Comms & Distress Protocols

Clear comms save ships. Use the exact designated callouts over voice channels during live hauling operations. Non-standard callouts during CODE ORANGE will be ignored.

"Comms Clear"
Silence all non-essential chatter. Commencing quantum jump or entering a dangerous landing zone. Vanguard calls this when destination is confirmed safe.
"Contact Front"
Hostile bogeys detected on radar. Hauler prepares emergency evasive maneuvers. Vanguard begins engagement. Asset does not deviate from course unless instructed.
"Package Secure"
All cargo successfully loaded and secured. Formation is ready to depart. Await Vanguard confirmation before initiating quantum jump.
"QUANT IN CARGO"
Quantanium extraction confirmed. Stability Timer starts NOW. All fleet members acknowledge and plot fastest route to refinery. No detours, no stops.
"CODE ORANGE"
Direct distress override. The ship is under active attack. This signal mandates that all available Jackals — Security or otherwise — immediately drop current tasks and jump directly to the Asset's beacon. No exceptions.
"HARD EXTRACT"
Situation is unrecoverable. Abandon cargo if necessary. All ships break formation and burn to nearest safe point independently. Regroup at Levski or GrimHEX.

Mining-Specific Callouts

"Rock Hot" — Rock is cracked and fragments are volatile. All non-miners stay clear of the extraction zone.
"Belt Clear" — Scanner shows no pirate signatures in the immediate asteroid field. Safe to begin extraction.
"Sadaryx Confirmed" — Sadaryx crystals located on Delamar/Breaker Station. Crafter role reports to collection point.
